# Introduction to Low-E Glass
Low-E glass, or low-emissivity glass, is a type of glass that has been designed to reduce heat transfer between the inside and outside of a building. This is achieved through a thin, metallic coating that is applied to the glass, which reflects radiant heat rather than absorbing it.
## How Low-E Glass Works
* Reduces heat gain in the summer by reflecting sunlight and heat away from the building
* Reduces heat loss in the winter by reflecting internal heat back into the building
* Can be used in a variety of applications, including windows, doors, and skylights
## Benefits of Low-E Glass
* **Energy Efficiency**: Low-E glass can help to reduce energy bills by minimizing heat transfer and reducing the need for heating and cooling.
* **UV Protection**: The metallic coating on low-E glass also helps to block UV radiation, which can cause fading and damage to furniture and carpets.
* **Noise Reduction**: Low-E glass can also help to reduce noise pollution by minimizing the amount of external noise that enters the building
## Conclusion
Low-E glass is a highly effective way to improve the energy efficiency of residential homes. By reducing heat transfer and minimizing the need for heating and cooling, homeowners can save money on their energy bills and reduce their environmental impact.
