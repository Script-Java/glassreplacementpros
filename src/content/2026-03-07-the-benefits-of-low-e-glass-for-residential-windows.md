# Introduction to Low-E Glass
Low-E glass, or low emissivity glass, is a type of energy-efficient glass designed to reduce heat transfer between the inside and outside of a building. This is achieved through a thin, metallic coating that reflects radiation, keeping your home warm in the winter and cool in the summer.
## How Low-E Glass Works
* Reduces heat loss in the winter by reflecting internal heat back into the home
* Minimizes heat gain in the summer by reflecting external heat away from the home
* Can be used in a variety of window types, including double-pane and triple-pane windows
## Benefits of Low-E Glass
* **Energy Efficiency**: Low-E glass can significantly reduce your energy bills by minimizing the need for heating and cooling.
* **UV Protection**: The coating on low-E glass also blocks ultraviolet (UV) rays, which can cause fading and discoloration of furniture and carpets.
* **Noise Reduction**: Low-E glass can help reduce outside noise, making your home a more peaceful and quiet space.
## Conclusion
Low-E glass is a valuable investment for any homeowner looking to improve the energy efficiency and comfort of their home. By understanding how low-E glass works and the benefits it provides, you can make an informed decision when it comes to replacing your residential windows.
