# Introduction to Double-Pane Windows
Double-pane windows, also known as insulated glass units (IGUs), consist of two panes of glass separated by a gap. This gap is usually filled with air or an inert gas, which acts as an insulator, reducing heat transfer between the inside and outside of your home.
## Benefits of Double-Pane Windows
The benefits of double-pane windows are numerous:
* **Energy Efficiency**: By reducing heat transfer, double-pane windows help to keep your home warm in the winter and cool in the summer, which can lead to significant savings on your energy bills.
* **Noise Reduction**: The gap between the two panes of glass acts as a sound barrier, reducing outside noise and making your home a more peaceful place to live.
* **Condensation Reduction**: The inert gas filling the gap between the panes reduces condensation, which can help to prevent mold and mildew growth.
* **Increased Comfort**: With a more stable indoor temperature, you can enjoy a more comfortable living space.
## How Double-Pane Windows Work
The science behind double-pane windows is simple: the gap between the two panes of glass reduces convection, which is the transfer of heat through the movement of fluids. By minimizing convection, double-pane windows minimize heat loss in the winter and heat gain in the summer.
