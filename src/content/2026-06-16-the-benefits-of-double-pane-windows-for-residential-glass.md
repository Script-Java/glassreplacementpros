# Introduction to Double-Pane Windows
Double-pane windows, also known as insulated glass units (IGUs), consist of two panes of glass separated by a gap. This gap can be filled with air or a gas, such as argon or krypton, which provides additional insulation.
## Energy Efficiency Benefits
* Reduced heat transfer: The gap between the panes reduces heat transfer, keeping your home warm in the winter and cool in the summer.
* Lower energy bills: By reducing heat transfer, double-pane windows can help lower your energy bills.
## Additional Benefits
* Noise reduction: The extra pane of glass and the gap in between can help reduce noise from outside.
* Condensation reduction: The gap between the panes can help reduce condensation, which can lead to mold and mildew growth.
## Conclusion
Double-pane windows are a great option for residential glass, offering numerous benefits, including energy efficiency, noise reduction, and condensation reduction.
